# Core Principles — Daoist Alchemy Levels

Extracted from "Alchemy of the Soul" materials.

---

## 1. Growth Is Step-Based

Transformation is not continuous, but step-based.

Each level has:
- its own state
- its own resource quality
- its own task

Movement happens level by level.

---

## 2. Resource Defines Reality

Life state is determined by resource level.

- low resource → collapse, illness, confusion
- medium resource → stability, first action
- higher resource → success, relationships, money
- very high resource → spiritual realization

---

## 3. Work Must Match Level

Each level requires a different intervention:

- no support → give support
- frozen → give warmth
- unstable → give structure
- weak expression → give expression
- rigid → give softness
- shy → give courage

Main rule:

Do not give the wrong resource for the level.

---

## 4. Cycle-Based Work

1 cycle = ~1 level

Typical cycle:
- duration: ~1 month
- format: ~2 sessions

This creates a structured transformation process.

---

## 5. Diagnosis → Next Step

The system is not for labeling.

It is for:

- identifying current level
- defining next level
- choosing correct intervention

---

## 6. Three Axes Of Growth

1. Body / Health (1–7)
2. Social / Success (8–14)
3. Spirit / Dao (15–18)

Development is sequential:

body → success → spirit

Skipping levels creates instability.

---

## 7. Resource Transitions

Key thresholds:

- below 3 → unstable survival
- 3–5 → stabilization
- 5–8 → expansion and success
- 8+ → scale and mission
- 9+ → spiritual alignment

---

## 8. Practical Meaning

The model allows:

- clear diagnosis
- structured programs
- predictable progression
- client expectations management
- integration with Wu Xing
- integration with psychodynamics

---

---

## 10. Main Rule

The goal is not to jump to high levels.

The goal is:

move one level up and stabilize it.
