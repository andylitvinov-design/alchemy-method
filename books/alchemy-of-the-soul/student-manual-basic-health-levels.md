# Student Manual — Work Focus On Basic Wu Xing Health Levels

This manual describes how the principle of work changes across the basic Wu Xing Health Levels.

Important:
Each level differs radically from the others.
It is not only that different remedies or potencies may be used. The whole principle of work changes.

This document is for method students and practitioners. It is not a medical protocol and does not replace appropriate medical care.

---

## Core Principle

Correct intervention depends on the current resource level.

Wrong intensity can collapse the system.
Right intensity can stabilize and move the person one level upward.

The practitioner must track not only which remedy resonates, but also how the intervention affects the Wu Xing system.

---

## Level 1 — Storm / 0–2 Resource

### State

Energy is extremely weakened and chaotic.
The Wu Xing system cannot hold strong impulses.
Any strong intervention can be traumatic and may collapse the system.

### Main Risk

The elements may not withstand strong stimulation.

### Principle Of Work

Hold the spark of life.

Give the softest possible support, gradually and briefly, without allowing the fire to flare too strongly.

### Intervention Style

- very gentle
- minimal dose / minimal contact
- short exposure
- constant tracking
- support Water element
- do not stimulate strongly

### Possible Supports Mentioned In Source

- Bach anti-stress essence
- lavender oil
- chamomile oil
- homeopathy: Carbo vegetabilis in critical cases
- homeopathy: Ambra grisea

### Wu Xing Focus

Water.

### Main Task

Stabilize life and prevent collapse.

---

## Level 2 — Snow Queen

### State

There is first stability instead of chaos.
All elements feel frozen.
There are no feelings and no energy.

This is still a progress because the system is no longer in Storm.

### Main Risk

A strong impulse can move the system back into Storm.

### Principle Of Work

Melt the ice with warmth, but do not create a flood.

The Boat of Life — the container for elemental force — is not ready yet.

### Intervention Style

- warm gently
- do not overstimulate
- create a container in parallel
- build capacity before increasing force

### Possible Supports Mentioned In Source

Warmth / soul thawing:
- Saccharum

Container / boat-building:
- Graphites
- Magnesia muriatica

Food support:
- cranberry
- nuts

### Main Task

Warm the frozen system and create a container for force.

---

## Level 3 — Prometheus / 2.5–3.0 Resource

### State

Sparks of activity appear.
They are chaotic and unstable: sometimes strength is present, sometimes it disappears.

Reaching this level is already a major improvement.

### Principle Of Work

Strengthen and kindle the fire so the person feels:

"Yes, I am. I exist. I can."

### Intervention Style

- light tonics
- gentle strengthening
- support the first stable fire
- do not overload
- strengthen the feeling of I Am

### Possible Supports Mentioned In Source

Light tonics:
- ginger
- cinnamon
- rosehip

Homeopathy:
- Berberis

Food / resource:
- black sesame
- juniper berries

### Main Task

Grow inner strength and the feeling of existence.

---

## Level 4 — Fortress / 3.0–3.5 Resource

### State

First stability appears.
The person begins to manage life.
But stability is still limited.
The system does not fall, but it also does not move forward.

### Principle Of Work

Continue gentle background support while beginning to build bridges between elements and restart Qi movement through the Wu Xing cycle.

### Intervention Style

- keep supporting the base
- introduce movement gradually
- build bridges between elements
- center the system
- use structure and rhythm

### Possible Supports Mentioned In Source

Centering / grounding:
- root vegetables
- carrot
- beetroot

Mineral / crystal support:
- quartz

Homeopathic bridge remedies:
- Ruta
- Cocculus

Transition support:
- Magnesia muriatica
- glehnia root

### Main Task

Maintain support and start movement between elements.

---

## Level 5 — Lighthouse / 3.5–4.0 Resource

### State

Impulses appear not only to exist, but to contact the world.
The contact is still unstable.

### Principle Of Work

Strengthen the beginning of contact and breathing toward the world.

### Intervention Style

- light
- airy
- breathing-opening
- gentle social activation
- support first contact without pressure

### Possible Supports Mentioned In Source

- citrus oils
- honey
- fresh greens

### Main Task

Help the person begin contact with the world without losing stability.

---

## Level 6 — Captain / Warrior / 4.0–4.5 Resource

### State

Dignity, strength, and the ability to declare oneself appear.
The person can present themselves, but there may be fear of how they will be perceived.

### Principle Of Work

Strengthen determination, courage, clarity, and will.
Return the leadership position.

### Intervention Style

- strengthen will
- support clear presentation
- support courage
- work with fear of perception
- medium potencies may begin to be considered, depending on personal verification

### Possible Supports Mentioned In Source

- coffee
- spices
- homeopathy: medium potencies may begin here, for example 30C, only with personal verification

### Main Task

Return leadership position and capacity to act from self.

---

## Level 7 — Stream / 4.5–5.0 Resource

### State

This is a key level for softness, harmony, and flexibility in relationships with the external world.

The person has more strength, but now must learn flexible, soft, living contact.

### Principle Of Work

Develop softness, harmony, fluidity, and relational flexibility.

### Intervention Style

- soft
- fluid
- moisturizing
- harmonizing
- relational
- do not force scale too early

### Possible Supports Mentioned In Source

- aloe
- herbs
- fluid oils
- jojoba oil

### Main Task

Develop soft flexible exchange with the world.

---

## Senior Levels — River And Beyond / 5–10 Resource

From River level onward, the work enters the senior Wu Xing stages.

This is no longer only health level.
It becomes social and spiritual development:

- manifestation in the world
- realization
- love
- money
- leadership
- mission
- Dao connection

Senior levels should be described separately.

---

## Main Student Rule

At each level, the practitioner must ask:

1. Can the system withstand this intervention?
2. Which element is most fragile?
3. Which element needs support first?
4. Is the intervention too strong?
5. Does the remedy/tool increase resource or collapse the system?
6. What is the next level, not the ideal level?
