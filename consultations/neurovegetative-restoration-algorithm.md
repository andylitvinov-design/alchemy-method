# Neurovegetative Restoration Algorithm

This file describes the staged algorithm for restoring the neurovegetative structure of the brain in Andrey Li's method.

This is a symbolic, educational, and method-oriented framework. It does not replace medical, neurological, psychiatric, or psychological diagnosis or treatment.

---

## 1. Core Principle

The neurovegetative system restores itself by stages.

Do not work with more complex zones, such as cortex-level functions, while the basic brainstem levels are unstable.

Main rule:

lower levels first → higher levels later

In method language:

Do not work with the roof while the foundation is collapsing.

---

## 2. Two Models To Combine

This algorithm combines two related models:

### 1. DMNV Restoration Model — "Airplane"

This model describes stages of dorsal vagal / DMNV collapse and restoration.

It shows the depth of the fall:

- crash
- collapse
- brake
- fragile stability
- normal functioning
- strong support / overcontrol

### 2. Eight Gates Model

The 8 Gates model of neurovegetative psychosomatics describes key centers and gates that need correction.

When the models are combined, they form a practical personal correction algorithm:

current stage → depth of symptom → target gate → correction method

---

## 3. Purpose

The algorithm explains what to do in the moment based on:

- current stage
- symptom intensity
- depth of collapse
- which brain center/gate needs support
- what kind of correction is appropriate

The goal is to stabilize the system so that it collapses less often and recovers more reliably.

## 4. Eight Gates Overview

### Gate 1 — Life / DMNV

Theme:
life, survival, deep inhibition, freeze, shutdown

### Gate 2 — Comfort / NTS

Theme:
body sensations, physical state, comfort, bodily safety

### Gate 3 — Love / VV

Theme:
social engagement, warmth, safe connection, ventral vagal contact

### Gate 4 — Support / PAG

Theme:
protection, defensive strategies, basic support, fortress walls

### Gate 5 — Protection / PB

Theme:
threat evaluation, lighthouse, protective orientation

### Gate 6 — Power / LC

Theme:
conscious strength, vigilance, mobilization, readiness

### Gate 7 — Clarity / DRN

Theme:
clarity of attention, stable mood, light of consciousness

### Gate 8 — Freedom / PFC

Theme:
thinking, analysis, choice, freedom, planning, self-regulation

## 5. Stages Of Restoration

## Stage 1 — DMNV-crash

Airplane image:
The airplane has landed/crashed.

State:
- no strength
- no tone
- breathing difficulties
- full symptom
- deep shutdown
- severe collapse of life tone

Focus of correction:

- Gate 1 — Life: DMNV
- Gate 2 — Comfort: NTS

Work principle:
Do not stimulate strongly. First restore minimal life support and bodily comfort.

Possible support direction:
- warmth
- soft grounding
- body contact with safety
- very gentle breath without force
- basic orientation to body sensations

---

## Stage 2 — DMNV-collapse

State:
- chronic powerlessness
- long-term exhaustion
- low vitality
- body may function, but life force is very weak

Focus of correction:

- Gate 2 — Comfort: NTS
- Gate 3 — Love: VV

Work principle:
After basic survival support, restore comfort and safe relational warmth.

Possible support direction:
- body comfort
- warmth
- soft social connection
- receiving support
- gentle practices that restore feeling of being held

---

## Stage 3 — DMNV-brake

State:
- freezing
- periodic symptoms
- system can function but often stops
- internal brake activates under stress

Focus of correction:

- Gate 2 — Comfort: NTS
- Gate 3 — Love: VV
- Gate 4 — Support: PAG

Work principle:
Support the body, relationship safety, and protective structures.

Possible support direction:
- orienting
- safe boundaries
- soft movement
- protective imagery
- support practices that reduce freeze

---

## Stage 4 — Fragile Stability

State:
- normal functioning only in good conditions
- the system works when environment is safe
- stress easily disrupts balance

Focus of correction:

- Gate 3 — Love: VV
- Gate 4 — Support: PAG
- Gate 5 — Protection: PB

Work principle:
Strengthen relational safety, support, and threat evaluation.

Possible support direction:
- social safety
- emotional regulation
- boundary work
- threat/orientation practices
- practices that help distinguish real danger from old reaction

---

## Stage 5 — Normal Functioning

State:
- general stabilization
- body and consciousness function normally
- person can move toward goals more effectively

Focus of correction:

- Gate 6 — Power: LC
- Gate 7 — Clarity: DRN

Work principle:
Increase effectiveness, conscious energy, and clarity.

Possible support direction:
- goal-oriented activation
- rhythm
- clear planning
- alertness without overload
- practices for attention and clarity

---

## Stage 6 — Strong Support / Overcontrol

State:
- strong support exists
- person may rely on overcontrol
- there is enough structure, but freedom is limited
- excessive effort may replace natural regulation

Focus of correction:

- Gate 7 — Clarity: DRN
- Gate 8 — Freedom: PFC

Work principle:
Move from overcontrol to freedom, choice, and higher regulation.

Possible support direction:
- cognitive flexibility
- choice
- planning without rigidity
- softening control
- integration of clarity and freedom

---

## 6. How To Use In Session

On a personal session, clarify:

1. Current stage.
2. Depth of collapse.
3. Which gates are active or weak.
4. Which support is needed now.
5. Which practices or remedies match the stage.
6. How to prevent repeated collapse.

Session formula:

stage → gates → correction focus → body support → next stabilization step

---

## 7. How To Use During Symptom Drop

If there is a drop, collapse, or turbulence:

1. Identify the stage.
2. Do not jump to higher-level work.
3. Support the gates corresponding to the depth of the drop.
4. Stabilize the lower foundation first.
5. Move upward only after stabilization.

Example:

If the person is in DMNV-crash, do not start with PFC planning or big cognitive analysis. Start with Gate 1 and Gate 2: life and comfort.

## 10. Safety Boundaries

This model is not a medical neurological assessment.

It is a method map for understanding state, collapse depth, and support focus.

If a client has serious breathing problems, neurological symptoms, psychiatric emergency, severe medical symptoms, or acute crisis, recommend appropriate professional care.

---

## 11. Main Rule

Stabilize the foundation before working with higher functions.

Do not force clarity, goals, or cognitive work when the life-support system is in collapse.

Correct sequence:

Life → Comfort → Love → Support → Protection → Power → Clarity → Freedom
