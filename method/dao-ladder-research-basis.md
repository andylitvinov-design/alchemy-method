# Dao Ladder Research Basis

This file explains where the Dao Steps / Dao Resource Ladder come from and how the model is validated in practice.

The Dao Steps are not presented as abstract theory. They are a practical diagnostic and transformation model.

---

## 1. Theoretical Base

The Dao Steps are based on two main theoretical foundations:

### Wu Xing Theory

The Daoist model of five elements / Wu Xing and their stages:

- depletion
- overheating / excess
- balance
- movement between phases

Wu Xing helps describe how resource behaves inside the system:

- where it is depleted
- where it is frozen
- where it is excessive
- where it is balanced
- what movement is needed next

### Levels Of Health

The model also draws from the concept of Levels of Health in homeopathy, especially the idea that a person's state can be understood as a level of vitality, reactivity, stability, and potential for recovery.

This gives the method a way to think not only in symptoms, but in levels of health and resource.

---

## 2. Personal Diagnosis

The balance of elements can be measured and diagnosed.

The strength of Wu Xing elements in a person reflects:

- current state
- health resource
- social potential
- spiritual potential
- capacity for change

Diagnosis can be done through:

- personal energetic / field diagnosis
- health-state tests
- Water test
- Metal test
- direct inquiry and observation
- client report data

The goal is to identify where the person is now and what resource movement is needed.

---

## 3. Element Level System

Through field/constellation work, it is possible to clarify the key qualities and standards of each new Wu Xing level.

The dynamics of elements reflect the levels of inner alchemy:

- Dao Steps
- Wu Xing Dao Steps
- resource levels
- stages of inner alchemy

This means that each level is not just a number.

Each level contains:

- a quality
- an archetype
- a task
- a challenge
- a method of work
- a resource that must be restored
- a next-level state

## 5. Practice Validation

The model is checked through real client work.

In practice, clients can be guided through the Dao Steps / Levels of Health model.

For each person, it is possible to observe:

- current level
- current Wu Xing balance
- what remedies resonate at this stage
- what naturopathic tools resonate
- what oils or support tools resonate
- what changes when the person moves to the next level

This practical observation gives additional validation and deeper understanding of each Dao Step.

---

## 6. Resulting System

The model forms a coherent system:

- 7 health levels / resource 0–5
- 10 social and spiritual development levels / resource 5–10

This creates a practical and measurable structure.

The method can show:

- current state
- current resource level
- effect of support
- movement between stages
- changes in remedies or tools after transition
- next realistic step

---

## 7. Personal Verification Rule

Even if the ladder gives a general map, each person's health level and Wu Xing correction method must be checked personally for maximum effect.

Do not apply the level mechanically.

Always verify:

- actual client state
- Wu Xing balance
- body signals
- emotional signals
- remedy resonance
- practical response
- level transition over time

---

## 8. Main Principle

The Dao Resource Ladder is practical because it allows the practitioner to measure state and effect.

It connects:

Wu Xing → Levels of Health → field diagnosis → archetypal model → remedy/support resonance → practical client change

The value of the model is not only explanation.

The value is measurable transformation.
